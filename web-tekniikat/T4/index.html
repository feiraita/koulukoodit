Basics

The Van cat is a distinctive landrace (or "natural breed") of the domestic cat found around Lake Van in the Eastern Anatolia Region of Trukey.
Van cats are relatively large, have a chalky white coat, sometimes with ruddy coloration on the head and hindquaters, and have blue or amber eyes or have heterochromia (one eye of each color).
The variety has been referred to as "the swimming cat", and has been observed to swim in Lake Van.

Characteristics

Van cats are all-white, or sometimes mostly white and amber marking around the tail and ears. Locals to the Van area identify only the all-white type as Van cats, according to a 1991 BBC documentary, Cats, written and presented by Roger Tabor.
